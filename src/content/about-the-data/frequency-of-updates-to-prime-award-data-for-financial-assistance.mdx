All agencies must submit financial assistance awards to the DATA Act
Broker within two weeks of issuance, as required by the
<a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Implementation-Guidance-for-Supplemental-Funding-Provided-in-Response.pdf">Office of Management and Budget (OMB) Memorandum M-20-21</a>.
Once new data are published in the Broker, they appear on
USAspending.gov the following day.

-   Example: A grant is issued on September 8. It must be reported
    to the Broker within 14 days, by September 22. If submitted on
    September 22, the new data are published on USAspending.gov on
    September 23.

The exception is loan awards, which must be published within 30 days, following the general requirements of the Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act of 2006 (FFATA)
 (<a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=(title:31%20section:6101%20edition:prelim)%20OR%20(granuleid:USC-prelim-title31-section6101)&f=treesort&edition=prelim&num=0&jumpTo=true">31 USC ยง 6101 note</a>).

-   Example: A loan is issued on September 8. It must be published on
    USAspending.gov by October 8 and thus must be submitted to the DATA
    Act Broker by October 7.
