If you’re passionate about a cause—whether it’s education, environmental protection, public health, or national security—you can use [Advanced Search](/search) to explore exactly how the U.S. government spends money on a particular topic.

[USAspending.gov](https://www.usaspending.gov) is the official source for federal spending powered by open data from awarding and funding agencies. USAspending provides easy access to detailed information on how taxpayer dollars are spent, allowing you to explore where money is going, who is receiving it, and which federal agencies are involved.

Here’s how you can use USAspending’s Advanced Search to track spending on the issues you care about.

![Discover How Government Spending Supports Causes You Care About](discover-how-government-spending-supports-causes-you-care-about.png)

## Search by Keywords

Add a keyword, like “climate,” “opioid,” or “STEM education”, into the keyword search field and click submit. This brings up federal contracts, grants, and loans related to your chosen topic.

## Explore by Agency or Location

Use filters to narrow results by agency (like the [Department of Defense](/agency/department-of-defense) or the [Department of Health and Human Services](/agency/department-of-health-and-human-services)) or by your [state or county](/search?hash=be10ddb9cd910b2d958b5118a9c7b5c9&section=map). This helps you understand how national spending priorities impact your local community.

## Track Spending Over Time

Search for spending data by period of performance or [fiscal year](https://usaspending.gov/search?hash=03c17c8b96d80e1ce20512d96358d054&section=time&by=fiscal_year). Select multiple options to view results over time and compare spending year-over-year.

## Follow the Money to Recipients

Each search result links to detailed profiles of [recipients](/featured-content/data-definitions/what-is-a-recipient)—whether it’s nonprofits, businesses, or universities—so you can see who’s receiving funding to do the work you care about.



Whether you’re a student, researcher, or simply curious,[USAspending.gov](https://www.usaspending.gov) gives you the power to follow the money and see how federal investments align with your personal priorities. It’s government transparency—powered by open data and made for people like you.
