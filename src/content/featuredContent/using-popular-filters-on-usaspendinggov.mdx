Federal spending touches nearly every community in the nation—but with billions of records available, finding the details that matter to you can feel like looking for a needle in a haystack. 

USAspending’s [Advanced Search](https://www.usaspending.gov/search) feature is designed to change that. With a set of powerful filters, you can pinpoint exactly where and how federal dollars are spent. 

![Using Popular Filters on USAspending.gov](using-popular-filters.png)

Here’s how some of the most popular filters work: 

- Keyword: Enter a word or phrase, such as “renewable energy,” “STEM education,” or “highway construction,” to quickly locate spending tied to specific programs, topics, or project descriptions.
- <GlossaryLink term="recipient" label="Recipient" />: Search for awards made to a particular organization, company, university, or government entity. This is especially useful for understanding who is receiving federal contracts or grants.
- <GlossaryLink term="fiscal-year" label="Fiscal Year" />: Narrow results by the year in which funds were obligated. Tracking by fiscal year helps you see year-over-year trends, shifts in priorities, or changes in funding levels.
- <GlossaryLink term="sub-award" label="Sub-award" />: Go beyond prime award data to see how recipients pass funding along to other organizations, revealing a fuller picture of how federal money flows.
- <GlossaryLink term="award-id" label="Award ID" />: Every federal award has a unique identifier. If you have this ID, you can jump straight to the details for that specific contract, grant, or loan. 

The real strength of Advanced Search lies in combining filters. For example, you might search for all “clean energy” awards in Fiscal Year 2024 made to [recipients](https://www.usaspending.gov/featured-content/data-definitions/what-is-a-recipient) in your state, then drill down to see which subrecipients benefit. Or you could use the <GlossaryLink term="award-id" label="Award ID" /> to confirm details you found in a news article.  

Whether you’re curious how federal spending affects you, researching a federal program, or checking facts for a story to report, Advanced Search makes big datasets easy to understand and use. 

Start exploring at [USAspending.gov/search](https://www.usaspending.gov/search) and discover how to turn federal spending data into knowledge you can use. Also, stay tuned for future articles offering a deeper dive on how to use various search filters.
