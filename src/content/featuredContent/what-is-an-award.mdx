When you think of the word “award,” you might picture an actor receiving an Oscar, an athlete earning a medal at the Olympics, or even yourself earning a gold star sticker from a teacher for a job well done.

<img src={Image} />

But in the world of government spending, “award” has a very specific meaning.
On USAspending.gov, an award is money the federal government has promised to pay a recipient. 

Money may be “awarded” to a company, organization, government entity (i.e., state, local, tribal, federal, or foreign), or individual. It may be <GlossaryLink term="obligation" label="obligated" /> (promised) in the form of a contract, grant, loan, insurance, direct payment, etc. 

To learn more about government spending, visit the <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.usaspending.gov/federal-spending-guide">Federal Spending Guide</a>.
